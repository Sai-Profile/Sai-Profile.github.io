Slide 2

Problem & Why It Matters

  • Work is fragmented → slow handoffs and fixes
  • Exam-time risks rise without clear procedures
  • Students/lecturers get an uneven experience
Fragmented workflows cause bottlenecks—especially during exams—so issues linger and experiences vary across courses.

“Work is fragmented—so fixes are slow, exam risks go up, and the experience is uneven.”

Slide 3

Aim & What You Get

  • Aim: describe LSC best practices in plain steps
  • Best-Practice Playbook — simple how-to pages
  • Process Maps + RACI — who does what
  • KPI Dashboard — quick view each term
Practical deliverables so operations run the same way every term: a concise playbook, process maps with clear roles, and a compact KPI view.

“Describe best practices and deliver a Playbook, process maps with RACI, and a termly KPI dashboard.”

Slide 4

Research Questions

  • What works best in our LSC?
  • How it runs: people, process, technology, policy
  • How we know it works: access, time, quality, training, integrity
  • Do we meet standards? CAS / CRLA-ITTPC / AUN-QA
We identify effective practices, examine the operating model, measure results, and compare to recognized standards.

“What works, how it runs, how we prove it works, and whether we meet CAS/CRLA-ITTPC/AUN-QA.”

Slide 5

Scope & Boundaries

  • Included: timetables & retakes; course specs & QA; materials; classroom/online setup; Google Classroom invites; announcements; exam setup & invigilation; grading/results/score-review; teaching feedback; tutoring/advising with CSSA (incl. ACCA/CAT top-scorers)
  • Not included: Registrar/Finance transactions (we map interfaces)
  • Ethics: site anonymized as “University A”
Focus is on LSC-coordinated services and handoffs with other units. Sensitive transactions remain out-of-scope.

“We cover LSC work and its links to other units—not Registrar/Finance transactions. The site stays ‘University A.’”

Slide 6

Design & Standards

  • Qualitative descriptive single-case (clarity, not cause–effect)
  • Compare to standards (descriptive): CAS • CRLA-ITTPC • AUN-QA
  • Purpose: find strengths & gaps — not score people
We document the real workflow and describe alignment to CAS, CRLA-ITTPC, and AUN-QA—not to grade individuals, but to improve systems.

“Qualitative descriptive single-case, compared to CAS/CRLA-ITTPC/AUN-QA, to find strengths and gaps—no scoring.”

Slide 7

Methods

  • Documents: policies, Standard Operating Procedures (SOP), logs
  • Observations: tutoring, classroom/tech setup, invigilation
  • People’s voice: interviews + Focus Group Discussions (FGD) with leaders/staff/lecturers/students/CSSA
  • Short student survey (optional): access, strategies learned, satisfaction
Mixed qualitative sources: document review, structured observations, stakeholder interviews/FGDs, and a short optional student survey for breadth.

“Read docs, observe tutoring/setup/invigilation, interview/FGD stakeholders, and run a short student survey.”

Slide 8

What We Measure (KPI)

  • Access & Time: reach; wait-time; on-time starts; results on time; score-review within SLA 7 days
  • Quality & Training: average tutoring rubric score; % tutors trained; observations per tutor
  • Integrity & Records: invigilator:student ratio; incidents/1,000; posting accuracy; paperwork complete
KPIs summarize how many/how fast (Access & Time), how good/how prepared (Quality & Training), and how fair/how accurate (Integrity & Records).

“Access & Time (how many/how fast), Quality & Training (how good/how prepared), Integrity & Records (how fair/how accurate).”

Slide 9

Feasibility, Risks, Timeline

  • Ready to start: tools drafted; calendar set; privacy plan ready
  • Risks → fixes: exam weeks (pre-book + backups); low response (multi-channel invites); sensitive data (role-based access + redaction); tech issues (IT/LMS backups)
  • 12-week plan: W1 ethics → W2–3 pilot → W4–7 data → W8 close survey → W9–10 analysis & maps → W11–12 playbook & report
A lightweight 12-week schedule with safeguards for peak exam periods, data sensitivity, and platform risks ensures steady progress.

“Risk plan: book early + backups (exam weeks); invite on many channels (low response); limit access + remove names (sensitive data); paper/second platform ready (tech issues).”

  • Next-term wins: more on-time starts; results & reviews meet SLA; integrity-safe tutoring
  • Leaders use a KPI dashboard — improve each term; aligned to standards (CAS • CRLA-ITTPC • AUN-QA)
Approval enables a short pilot and quick wins next term, with a leadership dashboard to drive continuous improvement against standards.

“If approved, the study will deliver visible next-term outcomes—on-time operations, 7-day score-review compliance, integrity-safe tutoring, and a KPI dashboard aligned to CAS/CRLA-ITTPC/AUN-QA.”
