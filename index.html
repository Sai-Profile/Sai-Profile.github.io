Reimagining University Administration: Learning Support Center (Phnom Penh)

A descriptive single-case study from a private university in Phnom Penh, Cambodia

  • Reimagining University Administration — our proposal
  • Best Practices in the Learning Support Center (LSC) — Phnom Penh, private university
  • Descriptive single-case study — clear, reusable, scalable
We set out a practical model for how an LSC can run day-to-day and still meet quality benchmarks. The study distills what works into a simple format other campuses can adopt.

“We’ll clearly show what works in the Learning Support Center and turn it into a reusable, scalable plan.”

Slide 2

Problem & Why It Matters

  • Work is fragmented → slow handoffs and fixes
  • Exam-time risks rise without clear procedures
  • Students/lecturers get an uneven experience
When tasks live in separate inboxes and ad-hoc chats, bottlenecks appear—especially during exams. Students and lecturers notice inconsistency across courses and terms.

“Work is fragmented—so fixes are slow, exam risks go up, and the experience is uneven.”

Slide 3

Aim & What You Get

  • Aim: describe LSC best practices in plain steps
  • Best-Practice Playbook — simple how-to pages
  • Process Maps + RACI — who does what
  • KPI Dashboard — quick view each term
Deliverables help operations run the same way every time: a concise playbook, visual process maps with RACI roles, and a small dashboard (e.g., access time, service quality, training, integrity).

“Describe best practices and deliver a Playbook, process maps with RACI, and a termly KPI dashboard.”

Slide 4

Research Questions

  • What works best in our LSC?
  • How it runs: people, process, technology, policy
  • How we know it works: access, time, quality, training, integrity
  • Do we meet standards? CAS / CRLA-ITTPC / AUN-QA
We examine practices, operating model, and evidence of results. We also compare against established standards to spot strengths and gaps.

“What works, how it runs, how we prove it works, and whether we meet CAS/CRLA-ITTPC/AUN-QA.”

Slide 5

Scope & Boundaries

  • Included: timetables & retakes; course specs & QA; materials; classroom/online setup; Google Classroom invites; announcements; exam setup & invigilation; grading/results/score-review; teaching feedback; tutoring/advising with CSSA (incl. ACCA/CAT top-scorers)
  • Not included: Registrar/Finance transactions (we map interfaces)
  • Ethics: site anonymized as “University A”
We focus on services coordinated by the LSC and its handoffs with other units. Sensitive administrative transactions are out-of-scope; we only define the touchpoints.

“We cover LSC work and its links to other units—not Registrar/Finance transactions. The site stays ‘University A.’”

Slide 6

Design & Standards

  • Qualitative descriptive single-case (clarity, not cause–effect)
  • Compare to standards (descriptive): CAS • CRLA-ITTPC • AUN-QA
  • Purpose: find strengths & gaps — not score people
We document how the LSC operates in practice, and compare it descriptively with CAS, CRLA-ITTPC, and AUN-QA criteria. The objective is improvement, not performance grading.

“Qualitative descriptive single-case, compared to CAS/CRLA-ITTPC/AUN-QA, to find strengths and gaps—no scoring.”

Slide 7

Methods

  • Documents: policies, SOPs, logs
  • Observations: tutoring, classroom/tech setup, invigilation
The case uses document review (policies, SOPs, operational logs) and structured observation of real activities (tutoring, room/tech readiness, and exam operations).

“We review documents and observe tutoring, classroom/tech setup, and invigilation in practice.”
